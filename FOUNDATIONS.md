# U.S. Foundation & Society Funding for Computational, Blood-Cancer & Rare-Disease Research

> A curated catalog of **non-federal** U.S. funders (foundations, professional societies, and industry) that support research in **AI / computational biology**, **blood cancers / hematologic malignancies**, and **rare hematologic disease (MDS, bone-marrow failure, aplastic anemia)** — assembled for a **senior postdoc** planning the transition to independence.

**How to use this file**

- This is a companion data source to the **Grantboard** dashboard (`index.html`). The same programs are searchable and rank-matched against your CV there; this document is the human-readable reference.
- Entries are grouped by theme. Each carries an **award ceiling, duration, deadline pattern, eligibility, and a direct link**.
- ⭐ marks **postdoc→faculty transition awards** — the highest-leverage targets for a senior postdoc (they either fund an advanced postdoc period or bridge into a first faculty appointment).
- 🎓 marks programs **open to postdocs / research fellows** (vs. faculty-only).
- ⚠️ **Always confirm the current cycle, amount, and eligibility on the funder's own page** — deadlines and figures shift yearly. Confidence flags (`high`/`med`/`low`) reflect how firmly each figure was verified at compile time.

**Coverage:** 127 non-federal programs across 69 funders. A federal-transition appendix (F32, K99/R00, K22, K25, DP2/DP5, NCI F99/K00) follows at the end.

## For a senior postdoc: where to focus first

A senior postdoc with a strong record in computational oncology / blood cancer should prioritize, roughly in order:

1. **Independent postdoctoral fellowships that travel with you** — Damon Runyon (incl. the *Quantitative Biology Fellowship* for computational scientists), Jane Coffin Childs, Helen Hay Whitney, Life Sciences Research Foundation, HHMI Hanna Gray, Cancer Research Institute, ACS Postdoctoral Fellowship, LLS Career Development *Fellow*. These fund the remaining postdoc years and strengthen the faculty package.
2. **Postdoc→faculty transition awards** ⭐ — NIH K99/R00 (federal, see appendix), Burroughs Wellcome *CASI* (built for quantitative/computational scientists entering biology), ASH *Scholar Award*, LLS *Career Development Program – Scholar/Special Fellow*, Damon Runyon *Dale F. Frey / physician-scientist* tracks. Apply while still a postdoc; the award activates as you start a lab.
3. **Disease-specific society awards** — ASH, LLS, MMRF, Lymphoma Research Foundation, Leukemia Research Foundation: smaller pools, high topical fit, and they build your standing in the hematology community.
4. **Rare-disease funders** (MDS / bone-marrow failure) — EvansMDS, MDS Foundation, AAMDSIF, Taub, Dresner: low competition and a natural fit if any of your work touches MDS/BMF.
